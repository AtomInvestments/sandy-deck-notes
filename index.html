ii. Slide by slide

What each slide does to the story.

All twelve
  • 01.
    Cover · "The AI cure for healthcare's admin overload"
    Sandy logo. "AI cure for healthcare's admin overload" tagline.
    REWRITE
    The problem

    "AI cure for admin overload" is the same opener every healthcare-AI deck on the planet uses. "Cure" doesn't differentiate. A CFO has seen 40 of these.

    EHR-agnostic revenue intelligence for hospitals that can't risk another vendor war. Lead with the positioning, not the metaphor.
  • 02.
    "Healthcare Has a Hidden Crisis"
    $150B+ losses, 15-20% claim rejections, burned-out staff bullets.
    CUT 40%
    The problem

    The stats are correct. The slide is generic. Every CFO knows the $150B number. You're spending a slide telling them their own pain — they wanted you to spend it telling them what you see they don't.

    Replace with the NYC $300K appeal-window-closed story. One specific scar beats four generic stats.
  • 03.
    "Luckily, There's a Cure" · Meet Sandy
    "She works the night shift (and the day shift, too)." Personifies the AI.
    KILL
    The problem

    Cutesy. Personifying the AI as a female employee named "Sandy" creates brand confusion (Sandy = company AND AI worker AND product?) and reads as consumer pitch energy in front of a hospital CIO. Burned-out staff don't want "a new teammate" — they want fewer denials.

    Replace with a single sentence: "Sandy is the revenue intelligence layer for pre-care." Done.
  • 04.
    Mission slide (black)
    "The only AI platform for healthcare that connects every pre-care workflow, powering patient access, revenue, and outcomes."
    PICK ONE
    The problem

    Three nouns at the end — access, revenue, outcomes — means you're still hedging on what Sandy IS. You said it yourself in the pitch: revenue intelligence. The other two are downstream effects, not the wedge.

    Cut "patient access" and "outcomes." Land on revenue. Promise one thing, deliver it, then expand the story in person.
  • 05.
    "Start Upstream & Everything Downstream Improves"
    Pre-care (Scheduling, Intake, Eligibility, Auths) → Care → Post-care flow.
    CONTRADICTS
    The problem

    Pre-care bullet #1 is Scheduling. You said in the meeting: "we don't build scheduling." Bruce was right. The slide directly contradicts your own scope statement — a CIO who's seen both will catch it.

    Drop "Scheduling" from the Pre-care column. Replace with Coverage verification or Eligibility intelligence.
  • 06.
    "Trust Isn't an Add-On"
    HIPAA, SOC 2, EHR/HMS integration, auditability bullets.
    PROMOTE
    The problem

    The bullets are correct. What's missing is the line that outweighs all six of them: "AWS wanted to buy our de-identification IP." That single sentence beats every checkbox on the slide. It's nowhere.

    Add a top callout: "AWS offered to acquire our de-id IP. We kept it." Move this slide from position 6 to position 2.
  • 07.
    "The AI Operating Layer for Healthcare"
    Patient-Centric, Open Core/Open Ecosystem, Plug-and-Play bullets.
    CUT
    The problem

    "Architected to unlock AI-native operations for the entire healthcare ecosystem" is consultant-speak. The phrase "operating layer" is what a16z writes about Snowflake — it doesn't survive a CFO's first question.

    Cut the slide. The positioning belongs in the cover line, not buried as slide 7.
  • 08.
    "Where Sandy Sits Today"
    Multiple pilots scheduled. Active discussions with hospital systems nationwide.
    NAME NAMES
    The problem

    "Multiple pilots scheduled" and "active discussions with leading hospital systems nationwide" reads as vaporware to anyone who's been pitched twice. The Texas 22-clinic deployment is real, proven, and quantified — that's the slide.

    Replace with: "Texas, 22 clinics, 1,000-patient roster, month one: 1.5 days + Saturday returned. Three more pilots Jan 2026." Specific beats sweeping.
  • 09.
    "The Humans Behind the AI"
    Diego (3 exits — Google, 8x8, Snowflake; Rad AI; MIT). Alex (3 M&A — Fuze, Jambb; Harvard MBA). Logo wall.
    MOVE FORWARD
    The problem

    The content is the strongest founder slide possible. 3 exits + MIT + Snowflake + Rad AI. But it sits at slide 9. Pre-seed, founder credibility is your moat — bury it at slide 9 and the CFO has already decided. The CrossFit and Reuters logos add noise next to AWS, Google, Rad AI.

    Move to slide 3 or 4. Strip CrossFit + Reuters. Lead bios with the exits, not "deep history and shared experience."
  • 10.
    "A Flagship Use Case at National Scale"
    500+ locations, 2M patients/month, 8-figure annual potential, VoB → prior auths expansion.
    PROMOTE
    The problem

    This is the slide that should make a CFO lean forward. 500 locations, 2M patients/mo, 8-figure ARR potential — and it's at slide 10. By the time you reach it, the room has already formed an opinion on the metaphor + the cutesy AI persona.

    Move to slide 5 (after the team slide). Add the client logo if NDA permits — anonymized is fine.
  • 11.
    "What Sandy Looks Like in Practice (Book a Demo!)"
    Tiny dashboard screenshot.
    UNREADABLE
    The problem

    The screenshot is so small no one in a hospital boardroom can read it. You apologized for slides not rendering on the call — this is one of them. A dashboard the CFO can't read is worse than no dashboard.

    Replace with one zoomed callout: the variance flag that caught the $300K NYC underpayment. One number, one slide. The dashboard goes in the demo, not the deck.
  • 12.
    "Thank You!" / contact slide
    Logo + URL + email.
    BIGGEST MISS
    The problem

    You absorbed two hours of feedback and closed with "Thank you" and an email. No ask. No three names. No specific intro request. The room handed you the right to ask and you walked away with a website URL.

    Replace with three asks: "Two intros to revenue cycle leaders we haven't spoken to. One CISO who'll grade our de-id IP. Names within 48 hours." Same energy you brought to slide one.

iv. Self-contradictions

Where the deck argues with itself.

Three places
i.
Scheduling, both ways

Slide 5 includes Scheduling under pre-care benefits. You said on the call you don't build scheduling. A buyer who has heard both will catch the leak.

ii.
"Operating layer" vs "revenue intelligence"

The mission slide says "AI platform... access, revenue, outcomes." Slide 7 elevates it to "AI operating layer for healthcare." Your own positioning in the call was revenue intelligence. Three positionings in one deck.

Cute AI persona vs CFO buyer

Slide 3 personifies Sandy as a teammate who works the night shift. Slide 6 sells SOC 2 / HIPAA / hospital IT review. The audiences don't match — the cutesy version turns off the buyer the trust slide is for.

Move "Humans Behind the AI" to slide 3. Move "Trust" + the AWS line to slide 2. Move "Flagship Use Case" to slide 5. Kill "Operating Layer." Replace "Thank You" with three asks. Everything else can wait.
